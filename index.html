Hi! My name is Joanne Sullivan,
and I am a web developer.

I'm based in Boise, Idaho, which pretty much means that I couldn't help but be drawn to the outdoors. I love to camp, hike, bicycle, and I'm just starting to fish. Being outdoors is not the only thing I love. I also love to code.

My journey into tech began several years ago when I was working for a major technology company. I happened to be a part of a team that needed an intranet site, and I was in a position to take it on. I had development tools to use, but that didn't stop me from looking at the source code and figuring out what all of those mysterious tags were doing. After I left that job, I got away from web development, but that doesn't mean my fascination faded.

In September, 2016, I completed Skillcrush's Front-End Web Developer Blueprint where I updated my HTML skills and learned CSS and JavaScript/JQuery. Skillcrush also introduced me to many of the tools I continue to use, such as GIT and GitHub.

What I do

My focus is on writing clean, well-formatted, semantic HTML by hand. This allows me to ensure that every line I write is easy to read, easy to troubleshoot, and accessible. My CSS is well-organized, easy to read and easy to build on.

You can trust me to take your designer's PSD and quickly and easily convert it into a webpage that is a pixel-perfect match.
